# Findings Summary
## Avian Organoid Transcriptomics Reanalysis — GSE283090

**Author:** Joy Manyasi Kabaka | ORCID: 0000-0002-2253-3970  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Dataset:** Sun et al., 2025, Roslin Institute (GSE283090)

---

## What This Reanalysis Does

This reanalysis takes the most comprehensive public avian intestinal 
organoid single-cell transcriptomic dataset available (GSE283090) and 
interrogates it through a new lens asking not just what genes are 
expressed, but whether the White Leghorn-derived reference genome used 
for alignment conceals functionally important variation at loci relevant 
to pathogen susceptibility and immune response.

---

## Key Loci Interrogated

### 1. ANP32A — Host Restriction Locus
- ANP32A determines susceptibility to avian influenza (LPAIV)
- Variant p.N129D confers resistance in some breeds
- The White Leghorn reference genome carries one allele
- Broiler vs layer comparison in GSE283090 reveals whether 
  breed-specific expression differences exist even within 
  commercial breeds

### 2. Interferon-Stimulated Genes (ISGs)
- MX1, IFIT1, IFIT3, ISG15, OAS1, IRF7
- ISG profiles differ between broiler and layer epithelial cells
- This breed-level variation is masked when all data is 
  collapsed to a single commercial reference

### 3. Thermal Stress Genes
- HSP90AA1, HSPA8
- Relevant to kienyeji adaptation to East African climate
- Absent from standard avian organoid characterization studies

---

## The Kienyeji Gap Argument

The GSE283090 dataset compares only two commercial breeds 
(Roslin Broiler and Hy-Line Brown Layer). Even between these 
closely related commercial lines, significant cell-type-specific 
transcriptomic differences exist  particularly in epithelial cells.

East African kienyeji chickens represent a far more divergent 
genetic background, shaped by:
- Centuries of adaptation to thermal stress (>35°C)
- Endemic Newcastle Disease Virus (NDV) exposure
- Low-input, free-range smallholder production systems
- No artificial selection for commercial productivity traits

If broiler and layer differ this much, kienyeji would differ 
far more  yet no organoid model exists for any African indigenous 
breed. This is the gap this framework proposes to fill.
